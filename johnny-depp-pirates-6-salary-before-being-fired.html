    Back in the 1980s, a guy named Johnny Depp made his way into the entertainment industry, and he would spend the next several decades turning into one of the biggest and most bankable stars around.

    It all started on the small screen for the actor, as 21 Jump Street proved to be the perfect starring vehicle early on.     Johnny Depp's Time As Captain Jack Sparrow Made Him Millions

    2003's Pirates of the Caribbean: Curse of the Black Pearl was a surprise hit at the box office, and in the blink of an eye, Disney had a massive live-action franchise on its hands. It was truly a once-in-a-lifetime idea that worked, and Johnny Depp's Captain Jack Sparrow was the main attraction.

    Depp could not have been a better choice for the role, and he would star in a total of five Pirates movies that contributed to billions of dollars at the box office.

    Thanks to the success of the films and the popularity of Captain Jack Sparrow, Johnny Depp made a fortune.

    "For his first appearance as "Jack Sparrow" in 2003's "Pirates of the Caribbean: The Curse of the Black Pearl", Johnny earned $10 million. His base salary for the second "Pirates" installment was $20 million. With backend points her earned an additional $40 million for a total of $60 million. He earned a combined $55 million from the third "Pirates" movie," Celebrity Net Worth reports.

    A sixth Pirates movies has been in the worlds for some time, but alas, Depp will not be coming back for one last ride.

    Depp Was Set To Make Over $20 Million For 'Pirates 6'

    Fans are no doubt aware of the legal proceedings taking place between Johnny Depp and Amber Heard, as it's all news outlets want to speak about. A lot has been revealed, including the amount of money that Depp was supposed to make for a 6th Pirates of the Caribbean movie.

    According to Insider, "The manager, Jack Whigham, said in Depp's defamation trial against his ex-wife that Disney's movie chief Sean Bailey and "Pirates of the Caribbean" producer Jerry Bruckheimer verbally sealed a deal in around 2016 to pay Depp $22.5 million for the sixth movie in the series. But Depp never got to see those million. The sixth "Pirates" movie was never filmed, and Depp never got to reprise his role as Captain Jack Sparrow."

    That is an unthinkable amount of money to lose out on, and it's just one of many ways that the involved parties have been effected during this turbulent time.

    Depp wanted to reprise Captain Jack Sparrow one more time as a send-off, but this opportunity was taken from him.

    "My feeling was that these characters should be able to have their proper goodbye, as it were. A franchise can only last for so long and there's a way to end a franchise like that and I thought that the characters deserved to have their way out, to end the franchise on a very good note. I planned on continuing until it was time to stop," the actor said.
