    Diplo | Career

    Diplo and his other group member, DJ Low Budget, began producing mixtapes following the popularity of their parties.

    “Never Scared,” which was rated one of The New York Times’ top ten albums of 2003, is one of them.

    The group quickly gained a reputation for hosting events with guest performers.

    Both DJs grew more well-known in the local and national arena as they continued to host parties attended by celebrities such as Bun B and M.I.A.

    Diplo’s first solo album, “Florida,” was eventually released.

    Following the success of his Hooked on Hollertronix parties, Diplo expanded his business by establishing “The Mausoleum,” a video, recording studio, record label office, gallery, and event space in Philadelphia.

    Diplo began collaborating with M.I.A. in 2004. The two worked on the mixtape “Piracy Funds Terrorism Vol. 1” together.

    The album was named “Album of the Year” by both the New York Times and Pitchfork Media.

    Diplo went from obscure DJ to well-known producer, collaborating with artists such as Shakira, Bruno Mars, Snoop Dogg, and Die Antwood.

    Diplo began collaborating with a wider range of musicians, including Lil’ Jon, Gucci Mane, Rusko, and others.

    Justin Bieber, Lil Wayne, Travis Porter, Usher, and Britney Spears are among his most noteworthy production credits.

    In 2011, he co-founded Major Lazer with Swith. For its significant Jamaican dancehall elements, Major Lazer‘s debut album received critical acclaim.

    Diplo has also appeared in a number of movies and TV shows, including “The High Note” (2020), “Detective Pikachu” (2019), and others.
