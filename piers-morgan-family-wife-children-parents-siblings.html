    Piers Morgan is an English journalist, broadcaster, writer, and television host.

    He started his career on Fleet Street in 1988 with The Sun.

    Rupert Murdoch appointed him editor of the News of the World in 1994, when he was 29 years old, making him the youngest editor of a British national newspaper in more than half a century.

    Table of Content

    Piers Morgan ex-wife

    In 1991, Morgan married Marion Shalloe, a ward sister at a hospital.

    Albert, Spencer, and Stanley were the couple’s three kids, and they split in 2004 before divorcing in 2008.

    Piers Morgan wife

    He married journalist Celia Walden, the daughter of former Conservative MP George Walden, in June 2010. Walden gave birth to Elise, her first child and Morgan’s fourth, on November 25, 2011.

    Piers Morgan children

    Piers Morgan is the father of four children.

    With his first wife Marion Shalloe, the TV host had three sons: Spencer, Stanley, and Albert.

    Elise was born in 2011 after he married fellow journalist Celia Walden.

    Piers Morgan parents

    Piers was born in Surrey on March 30, 1965, to Vincent Eamonn O’Meara, a dentist from Galway, Ireland, and Gabrielle Georgina Sybille, an English Catholic.

    Vincent, who was only 11 months old when he died in a car accident, and his brother Jeremy, who was three, were the family’s only children.

    Piers Morgan siblings

    His brother is Jeremy Morgan.
