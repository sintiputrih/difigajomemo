    Kylie Jefferson Is 1994s Kid: Early Life

    Kylie Jefferson, aka Kylie Marie Jefferson, was born on 25 September 1994 in Los Angeles, California, the United States. 

    As of early 2021, Kylie Jefferson is 26 years old. 

    She holds an American nationality and belongs to the Afro-American ethnic background.

    Education

    Coming to her academic qualification, Kylie went to Debbie Allen Dance Academy (DADA), Culver City, California, the United States. She was directly instructed by the great Debbie Allen, a dancer, actress, choreographer, producer, and director. 

    She also joined the Boston Conservatory and graduated from there with a Fine Arts in contemporary dance Bachelor's degree in the year 2016. 

    Tiny Pretty Liars Actress Kylie Jefferson Career Highlights 

    While still studying at the Debbie Allen Dance Academy, Kylie Jefferson got an opportunity to perform in the Kennedy Center, Washington, D.C. Soon, she got to work in several commercials and infomercials. 

    Moreover, around her preteens, Jefferson went to both the Debbie Allen Dance Academy and Kirov Academy of Ballet, located in Washington, D.C.

    In 2019, Kylie debuted as a choreographer. She choreographed ScHoolboy Q's "CHopstix" feat. Travis Scott. For her choreography, she was acknowledged with a Universal Dance Awards nomination under Favorite Music Video Performance. She also choreographed dance performances on The Late Late Show with James Cordon, the 2020 GRAMMYS Awards, and NAACP Image Awards. 

    Eventually, in 2020, Kylie Jefferson debuted in the movie industry. Initially, she appeared as a featured dancer in Dance Dreams: Hot Chocolate Nutcracker. With her performance, she landed a role in the Netflix series Tiny Pretty Things as Neveah Stroyer alongside Brennan Clost, Michael Hsu Rosen, Ashley Coulson, Daniela Norman, Damon J. Gillespie, Barton Cowperthwaite, and others.
