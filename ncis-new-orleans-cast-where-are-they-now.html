    NCIS: New Orleans, the popular spin-off of the hit television series NCIS, ran for seven seasons before concluding in May 2021. The show, set in the vibrant city of New Orleans, followed a dedicated team of Naval Criminal Investigative Service agents as they solved crimes affecting military personnel. With its unique blend of mystery, drama, and local color, the series garnered a loyal fan base. Since the show’s finale, many fans have been curious about the whereabouts and activities of the cast members. Let’s delve into the lives of the NCIS: New Orleans cast and discover where they are now.

    Scott Bakula (Dwayne Pride)

    Scott Bakula, who led the cast as Special Agent Dwayne “King” Pride, has had a prolific career in television and film both before and after NCIS: New Orleans. Since the show ended, Bakula has been involved in various projects. He has made guest appearances on other TV shows and is rumored to be involved in upcoming film projects. Bakula, who is also known for his roles in Quantum Leap and Star Trek: Enterprise, continues to be a prominent figure in the entertainment industry.

    Lucas Black (Christopher LaSalle)

    Lucas Black, who played Special Agent Christopher LaSalle, left the show in its sixth season. Since his departure, Black has returned to the Fast & Furious franchise, reprising his role as Sean Boswell in F9. He has also spent time with his family and has been active on social media, sharing his passion for outdoor activities and his faith.

    Vanessa Ferlito (Tammy Gregorio)

    Vanessa Ferlito, who portrayed FBI Agent turned NCIS Agent Tammy Gregorio, has been relatively quiet since the series concluded. She has taken some time off but is expected to return to the screen with new roles in the near future. Ferlito has previously appeared in shows like 24 and Graceland, and her fans are eager to see what she will do next.

    Rob Kerkovich (Sebastian Lund)

    Rob Kerkovich, the actor behind Forensic Agent Sebastian Lund, has continued his acting career with guest roles on various television shows. Kerkovich is also known for his comedic skills and has been involved in writing and producing comedy sketches and shorts.

    CCH Pounder (Loretta Wade)

    CCH Pounder, the esteemed actress who played the wise and compassionate Medical Examiner Dr. Loretta Wade, has remained active in the industry. She has continued her role as a voice actress in the Avatar film series and has made appearances in other television projects. Pounder is also an art collector and philanthropist.

    Daryl “Chill” Mitchell (Patton Plame)

    Daryl “Chill” Mitchell, known for his role as the witty and skilled computer specialist Patton Plame, has been advocating for actors with disabilities through his work with the Daryl Mitchell Foundation. He has also continued acting, with guest spots on various TV shows.

    Shalita Grant (Sonja Percy)

    Shalita Grant, who played Special Agent Sonja Percy until the fourth season, has seen her career flourish post-NCIS: New Orleans. She has had a significant role in the Netflix series You and has been involved in other television and film projects. Grant is also an entrepreneur, having launched a haircare line.

    Necar Zadegan (Hannah Khoury)

    Necar Zadegan, who joined the cast as Special Agent Hannah Khoury in the fifth season, has continued to take on challenging roles in television and film. She has appeared in series such as The Good Doctor and has been involved in stage productions as well.

    Charles Michael Davis (Quentin Carter)

    Charles Michael Davis, who portrayed Special Agent Quentin Carter, has been active in the entertainment industry with roles in shows like Younger and For the People. Davis is also a model and photographer, showcasing his work on social media.

    Chelsea Field (Rita Devereaux)

    Chelsea Field, who had a recurring role as Rita Devereaux, has been spending time with her family, including her husband Scott Bakula. Field has had a long career in film and television and may return to acting in future projects.

    FAQ Section

    Has NCIS: New Orleans been renewed for another season?

    No, NCIS: New Orleans concluded with its seventh season and has not been renewed for an eighth season.

    Are any of the NCIS: New Orleans cast members working on new series?

    Yes, several cast members have taken on new roles in other television series and film projects since the show ended.

    Can fans expect to see any NCIS: New Orleans cast reunions in the future?

    While there are no official plans for a cast reunion, it’s not uncommon for actors who have worked together to collaborate again in the future or make guest appearances on each other’s projects.

    Conclusion

    The cast of NCIS: New Orleans has moved on to a variety of new endeavors since the show’s conclusion. From returning to beloved franchises to advocating for important causes, each actor has taken a unique path post-NCIS. While fans may miss seeing the team together solving crimes in the Big Easy, they can take comfort in knowing that the talented cast continues to thrive and entertain in new and exciting ways. Whether it’s through television, film, or other creative pursuits, the legacy of NCIS: New Orleans lives on through the ongoing work of its beloved cast.
