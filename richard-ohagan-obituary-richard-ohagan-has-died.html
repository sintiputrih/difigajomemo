    Richard O’Hagan Obituary, Death Cause – It is with deep sorrow that we must notify you of the following tragic news regarding the demise of a kind and compassionate spirit that was loved by many people in Longford and other places. The passing of Richard (Risteard) O’Hagan Cressil, who resided in Tydavnet, Monaghan, H18EY67; Athenry, Galway; and Longford; has taken place. The staff and management of The Longford Eye would like to offer their heartfelt condolences to the deceased person’s family and friends. We would like to extend an invitation to all of our readers to express their condolences and/or leave a comment below.

    Those who are suffering through their loss might find enormous solace in it. His father, Aidan, as well as his grandparents Liam and Noreen Hoare (Athenry), Joe and Anna O’ Hagan (Longford), and his uncle Michael Hoare (Swords), all passed away before him. His mother Mary, his sister Norrn, his companion Clare, Mary’s stepmother Evelyn, his uncles, aunts, cousins, extended relatives, friends, neighbors, and anybody else who knew and loved Richard will miss him terribly. On Thursday, the 12th, Richard’s family will receive visitors at their house (H18EY67) from 3 to 8 o’clock in the evening.

    The funeral procession for Richard will arrive at St. Mary’s Church in Urbleshanny, Scotstown, at 11 o’clock in the morning for the funeral liturgy. Following the service, burial will take place at the adjacent cemetery. Please only send flowers to the family. gifts in place of flowers can be made through the donation box at Clerkin Funeral Directors or directly to the “Kevin Bell Repatriation Trust” if preferred. “Funeral Arrangements Entrusted to Clerkin Funeral Directors” . Ar dheis Dé go raibh an n-anamacha, or “May Their Soul Rest in Peace,” is an Irish expression. (It is important to remember that even when funeral services are held in a location other than the house of the deceased, the property and its immediate surroundings may be observed for safety concerns.)
